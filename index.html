About me

I am currently pursuing a Master of Science in Computer Vision at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU). I have previously completed a Bachelor of Technology (Honours) in Computer Science and Engineering from the International Institute of Information Technology - Hyderabad (IIIT-H).

Broadly, my research and academic interests lie in Computer Vision and Machine Learning. My long term goal is to work on complex systems that match and surpass human vision in processing visual information in all aspects such as object detection, recognition, semantic understanding, 3D understanding, etc. I envision systems that can extract all relevant information from visual inputs, contextualize this information and use it in combination with other modalities to make appropriate inferences and decisions. Within Vision, I am particularly passionate to work on 3D reconstruction, implicit representations and the intersection of 2D and 3D information processing. I am also keen to explore the domain of generative models with a focus on the recent advances made with stable diffusion models. In pursuit of my interests, I am currently searching for internship opportunities for the summer of 2024. Feel free to reach out to me with any such opportunities or even just to have a stimulating discussion on my work and/or recent trends in the fields mentioned above.

  • RRC @ IIIT-H, IVL @ Brown

    Canonical Fields: Self-Supervised Learning of Pose-Canonicalized Neural Fields (CVPR 2023)

    Collaborated with Brown University to develop a pipeline, CaFi-Net, as a co-first author for self-supervised canonicalization of NeRF-generated fields in PyTorch-lighting; resulted in a publication in CVPR 2023 as a highlight paper. In this work, we extend the notion of equivariance to continuous vector fields and extend 3D point cloud networks to operate directly on NeRFs. We created and released a new dataset of 1300 pre-trained NeRF models of 13 common ShapeNet categories. This pipeline employed NeRFs, Tensor Field Networks (TFNs), spherical harmonics, and MLPs. This project was undertaken under the mentorship of Prof. Madhava Krishna (IIIT-H) and Prof. Srinath Sridhar (Brown).

  • RRC @ IIIT-H

    Pose-Shape Optimizer Pipeline

    Recreated a pipeline in MATLAB and Ceres that reconstructs and localizes a vehicle in 3D given a monocular 2D image. This involved deforming a 3D wireframe (obtained from vehicle shape priors using PCA) by estimating the pose and shape deformation such that the wireframe fit the vehicle key points when projected to 2D. Further applied multi-view optimization over the shape to stabilize it across sequences of video frames. Made use of a deep stacked hourglass CNN model to estimate vehicle key points in the images.

  • CMU

    Curiosity & Entropy Driven Unsupervised RL in Multiple Environments

    Developed a task-agnostic and partially environment-agnostic model that builds on top of previous work on unsupervised reinforcement learning driven by curiosity and entropy-based objectives. We employed an entropy-based objective, a curiosity-based objective, a dynamic percentile sampling of worst-performing trajectories for both objectives during optimization and a more fluid KL Divergence constraint TRPO model. Training our model involves two phases: the first phase is the unsupervised training phase using the two earlier-mentioned objectives to obtain a generic task-agnositc model while the second phase is the supervised training phase using environment-derived rewards to finetune the model for a specific task.
